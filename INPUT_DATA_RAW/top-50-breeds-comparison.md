## KEY CHARACTERISTICS EXPLANATION

### ENERGY LEVEL SCALE (1-10)
- **1-3**: Low energy (Bulldog, Pug, Shih Tzu, Basset Hound, Great Dane, Mastiff)
- **4-5**: Low-Moderate (Havanese, French Bulldog, Cavalier, Chihuahua)
- **6-7**: Moderate-High (Poodle, Pembroke Welsh Corgi, Miniature Schnauzer, Boxer, Rottweiler)
- **8-9**: High-Very High (Beagle, German Shepherd, Labrador, Australian Shepherd, German Shorthaired Pointer)
- **10**: Ultra High (Border Collie)

### INTELLIGENCE RANKING
1. Border Collie (extraordinary problem-solving)
2. Poodle (quick learning, adaptable)
3. German Shepherd (versatile, multi-purpose)
4. Labrador Retriever (eager to please)
5. Golden Retriever (intelligent, service dog capable)
6. Doberman Pinscher (alert, quick to learn)
7. Pembroke Welsh Corgi (smart, responsive)
8. Australian Shepherd (high drive)
9. Belgian Malinois (driven, alert)
10. Papillon (responsive, trainable)
11. Collie (intelligent, herding heritage)

### GROOMING SCALE (1-10)
- **1-2**: Minimal (French Bulldog, Bulldog, Pug, Boxer, Beagle, Dachshund)
- **3-4**: Low-Moderate (German Shepherd, Golden Retriever, German Shorthaired Pointer)
- **5-6**: Moderate (Poodle variants, Australian Shepherd, Miniature Schnauzer)
- **7-9**: High-Very High (Cavalier, Yorkshire Terrier, Shih Tzu, Poodle, Maltese)

### SHEDDING SCALE (1-10)
- **1-3**: Minimal (Poodle, Yorkshire Terrier, Maltese, Bichon Frise, Miniature Schnauzer)
- **4-6**: Moderate (Dachshund, Boston Terrier, Boxer, Cocker Spaniel)
- **7-9**: Heavy (Golden Retriever, German Shepherd, Labrador, Siberian Husky, Australian Shepherd)

### LIFESPAN PATTERNS
- **6-10 years**: Large/Giant breeds (Great Dane 7-10, Mastiff 6-10, Boxer 7-10)
- **10-13 years**: Most medium/large breeds (German Shepherd 9-13, Labrador 10-12)
- **12-16 years**: Most small breeds (Cavalier 12-15, Beagle 10-15)
- **14-18+ years**: Toy breeds (Chihuahua 14-18, Yorkshire Terrier 11-15, Shih Tzu 10-18)

### COST BREAKDOWN
- **$400-$800**: Uncommon/Rare breeds, some hounds
- **$800-$1,500**: Most common breeds (Labs, Goldens, German Shepherds)
- **$1,500-$2,500**: Popular breeds with higher demand
- **$2,500-$4,000+**: French Bulldogs, Bulldogs, rare colors, working lines

## NOTES & CAVEATS

1. **Individual Variation**: Not all dogs match breed averages perfectly
2. **Training Impact**: Separation anxiety and behavior issues can be improved with proper training
3. **Health**: Prevention (good nutrition, exercise, preventive vet care) reduces likelihood of health issues
4. **Cost Variation**: Regional differences (California 2.2x more expensive than Idaho) and breeders vary significantly
5. **Lifespan**: Lifestyle, healthcare quality, and luck all play roles
6. **Characteristics**: Based on breed standards; mixed breeds may differ
7. **Rescue/Adoption**: All these breeds available in shelters at fraction of breeder cost ($50-500 adoption fee vs. $800-4000 purchase)
