### HEALTH ISSUES COMPARISON

#### High Health Risk Breeds
- **Bulldog** - Brachycephalic airway (479% higher risk), respiratory disease, hip dysplasia
- **French Bulldog** - BOAS (479% higher), respiratory disease (479%), heat sensitivity (533%)
- **Boxer** - Cancer (highest risk), heart disease, bloat
- **German Shepherd** - Hip dysplasia (very common), IVDD, elbow dysplasia
- **Cavalier King Charles Spaniel** - Heart disease (MMVD), genetic load highest
- **Rottweiler** - Hip dysplasia, elbow dysplasia, bloat (life-threatening)
- **Dachshund** - IVDD (common, back issues), chondrodystrophy, degenerative myelopathy

#### Moderate Health Risk Breeds
- **Labrador Retriever** - Hip/elbow dysplasia, cancer, bloat
- **Golden Retriever** - Hip/elbow dysplasia, cancer, cruciate ligament injuries
- **Poodle** - Hip dysplasia, eye problems (PRA, cataracts)
- **Beagle** - Epilepsy, cancer, hypothyroidism
- **Cane Corso** - Hip dysplasia, elbow dysplasia, bloat
- **Doberman Pinscher** - Dilated cardiomyopathy (DCM), cancer, von Willebrand's disease

#### Lower Health Risk Breeds
- **Chihuahua** - Generally hardy, patellar luxation, heart murmurs
- **Shih Tzu** - Kidney disease, eye problems, generally hardy
- **Pomeranian** - Generally healthy, patellar luxation
- **Maltese** - Generally healthy, small breed issues
- **Pembroke Welsh Corgi** - IVDD risk, generally hardy otherwise
- **Yorkshire Terrier** - Generally healthy, patellar luxation, dental issues
- **Miniature Schnauzer** - Generally healthy, kidney stones risk

## KEY TAKEAWAYS: BREED COMPARISON INSIGHTS

1. **No "Perfect" Breed**: Every breed has trade-offs (size, energy, health, grooming, cost)

2. **Lifestyle Matching Critical**: Right breed for YOUR situation, not just popularity

3. **Energy Mismatch #1 Problem**: Most returns/rescues from energy level mismatch

4. **Health Issues Vary Widely**: Some breeds have significant health concerns; know them before buying

5. **Grooming/Shedding Often Underestimated**: High-grooming breeds expensive long-term

6. **Size Affects Cost**: Large/giant breeds 30-50% more expensive than small

7. **Popular ≠ Best for You**: French Bulldogs #1, but may not fit many lifestyles

8. **Small Dogs Live Longer**: Average 12-18 years vs. large breeds 8-12 years

9. **First-Time Owners Need Forgiving Breeds**: Labs, Goldens, Poodles more trainable

10. **Adoption Cheaper Alternative**: Same dog, $2,000-$3,000 less in initial cost
